1 Nephi 4
9 Laban was carrying a sword. I drew the sword from its sheath. The sword was splendid. The hilt was pure gold with exquisite workmanship. The blade was made from the most precious steel.
10 I felt compelled by the Spirit that I should kill Laban. I felt this powerful prompting, yet hesitated. Never had I killed a man. I hesitated because I did not want to kill him.
11 The Spirit reminded me that God has delivered Laban into my hands. I also reviewed what had recently transpired. Laban tried to kill me. Laban would not give us the family history. Laban stole our gold, silver, and precious things.
12 Again, the Spirit told me: “Kill him. God has delivered Laban into your hands.”
13 “God destroys the wicked to realize His righteous purposes. It is better than one man dies than a nation should fall away in unbelief.”
